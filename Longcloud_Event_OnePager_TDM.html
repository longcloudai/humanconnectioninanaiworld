The Q&A — TDM Transport
Paul Singh × Trent Montgomery
A digital transformation platform for a Waikato logistics leader
Transport & Logistics Waikato, New Zealand Office portal + driver app Real-time · one database tdmtransport.co.nz

TDM Transport had grown into a leading Waikato freight and heavy-haulage operator. Rather than wait for the pains of scale to bite, Managing Director Trent Montgomery brought in Longcloud to build a single platform connecting people, vehicles, customers and data in real time — a foundation for the next phase of growth.

The Challenge

  • No single view — fleet, jobs, customers and staff lived in separate places.
  • Live visibility — fleet and job status had to be pulled together, not seen live.
  • Driver-to-office — coordination relied on ad-hoc calls and messages.
  • Compliance — vehicle compliance and maintenance needed a structured process as the fleet grew.
  • Reporting — performance insight was assembled by hand, not on demand.

What We Built

  • Office portal — real-time dispatch calendar plus jobs, CRM, HRIS, fleet, maintenance, fuel & reporting. Microsoft 365 single sign-on, locked to the company domain.
  • Driver app — mobile-first; each driver sees only their jobs, submits photo proof of pickup & delivery, and chats live with the office, all tied to the job.
  • One real-time database — an office assignment appears on the driver's phone, and a completed delivery back in the office, with no refresh or phone call.
▲ One live view
Fleet, drivers, customers and jobs connected in real time.
▲ Lower risk
Automated COF, rego & service tracking flags overdue items.
▲ Faster reporting
Revenue, pipeline & utilisation on demand; export to Excel.
▲ Built to scale
One integrated platform ready for TDM's ongoing growth.
Under the hood: Next.js 16 (React, App Router) · Supabase PostgreSQL & Storage · Vercel edge hosting. Real-time multi-user sync over WebSockets with optimistic UI; two purpose-built sign-in paths (Microsoft SSO for office, dedicated login for drivers); secure photo proof-of-delivery with a code-enforced "no photos, no completion" rule; and a graceful offline fallback for daily depot use. What's next: AI-assisted scheduling, route & utilisation optimisation, and predictive maintenance.
